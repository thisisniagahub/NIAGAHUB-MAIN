# Decision Maker Skill

## Overview
Help make better decisions through structured analysis, frameworks, and objective evaluation.

## When to Use
- User faces a decision
- User asks should I or patut ke
- User comparing options
- User needs objective perspective
- User feels stuck

## Capabilities

### 1. Decision Analysis
- Pros and cons
- Weighted scoring
- Risk assessment
- Opportunity cost

### 2. Frameworks
- Eisenhower matrix
- 10/10/10 rule
- First principles
- Inversion thinking

### 3. Comparison
- Option comparison tables
- Feature matrices
- Trade-off analysis
- Best-case/worst-case

### 4. Thinking Partner
- Challenge assumptions
- Ask clarifying questions
- Play devils advocate
- Identify blind spots

## Commands

### Analyze
- analyze decision: [decision]
- pros cons of [option]
- compare [A] vs [B]
- should I [action]

### Frameworks
- eisenhower [tasks]
- 10/10/10 for [decision]
- first principles [problem]
- invert [goal]

### Strategic
- opportunity cost of [choice]
- risk analysis [decision]
- what am I missing
- blind spots check

## Decision Frameworks

### 10/10/10 Rule
How will I feel about this decision:
- 10 minutes from now?
- 10 months from now?
- 10 years from now?

### Eisenhower Matrix
|  | Urgent | Not Urgent |
|--|--------|------------|
| Important | DO | SCHEDULE |
| Not Important | DELEGATE | DELETE |

### First Principles
1. What do I know for sure?
2. What assumptions am I making?
3. What would I do if starting fresh?

### Inversion
Instead of how to succeed, ask how to fail - then avoid those.
